Course: Computer Science Principles
Project: Logo Quiz
Duration: September 18, 2018 - October 8, 2018

The objective of this project was to design an app through the MIT App Inventor software. Brainstorming went very well with both group members equally enthusiastic towards creating an app that would quiz players on different logos. Programming in App Inventor was where it got tough for us because of the odd syntax and my unfamiliarity towards it in general. It took a lot of trial and error, and our progress was slow at first. This process took large amounts of time and I had to take the initiative of organizing our priorities to meet the deadline. As other requirements of our project like the design notebook were getting caught up, me and my partner were able to confidently spend more time troubleshooting and improving the code. I began watching some youtube tutorials for inspiration and took the feedback from the beta walk very seriously . This is when our project began to take off in a positive manner. I improved at programming in APP Inventor and the development of our app was smoother and more enjoyable now. Changing to a multiple choice format after the beta was a great decision that eliminated many previous cons. In the final gallery walk, we observed a great improvement with the amount of positve feedback increasing and negative feedback decreasing. There were still some minor issues we could have fixed with more time, but we definitely gave it our best with our given knowledge and time.
